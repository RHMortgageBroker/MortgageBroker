What Determines Your Rate?

Market rates are a starting point. Your actual rate depends on several factors specific to you and your loan. Here's what lenders look at:

  • Credit score: Higher scores qualify for lower rates. Even a small score improvement can save thousands over the life of your loan.
  • Loan type: Conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, jumbo, and non-QM loans each carry different rate structures.
  • Down payment / LTV: A larger down payment typically means a lower rate and no mortgage insurance requirement.
  • Loan term: 15-year loans carry lower rates than 30-year loans, but come with higher monthly payments.
  • Property type: Primary residences, second homes, and investment properties are priced differently by lenders.
  • Points & fees: You can pay discount points upfront to buy down your rate. I'll show you the break-even math so you can decide what makes sense.
